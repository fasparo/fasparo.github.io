Race misunderstanding

2015-04-12

The competitive connotations of "race" and "survival of the fittest" are nothing but a double linguistic misunderstanding.

Biological "race" comes from the Latin for "root" (whereas the sporting "race" derives from a separate Germanic word, also related to the modern "rush"). And "fittest" in Darwin's "survival of the fittest" is simply an archaic use of "fit" in its sense "suited" or "adapted". That is: a surviving butterfly, or tree, or ape, will be the one which most fits its environment.
