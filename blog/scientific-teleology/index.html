Scientific teleology

2013-04-14

In the Middle Ages, people knew that the Earth was at the centre of the universe. A few hundred years ago, doctors still used bloodletting as a remedy. In the early 20th century, a lot of smart people were certain that the Earth was solid.

The historical trap of teleology – seeing past events as predetermined – surely applies also to science in the present. On questions of animal intelligence ("of course humans are the smartest"), toxicity ("it could not possibly be dangerous"), economics ("more growth is clearly needed") and countless others, the founding principle of science is easily forgotten. Everything is theory, and and every theory must be reassessed, again and again.
