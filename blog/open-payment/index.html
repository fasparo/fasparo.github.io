Open tip jar

2015-01-18

Journalism seems at last to be adapting to the internet without reservations. But now it must face another risk: the temptation of selling its soul to that cartel of US-based corporations and services and platforms whose names do not need to be mentioned.

For an optimist, the destiny of every corporate web service is to be replaced by an open standard.

One standard surely waiting to happen is a P2P tip jar, to enable anonymous commission-free micropayments by consumers to content producers. Perhaps, as pioneered by Flattr, the payments could be aggregated and calculated monthly according to a predefined budget. Or they could simply be one-off donations as in the model being tried by YouTube. But, thanks to Bitcoin or some future successor crypto-currency, no Californian cloud-corporation would need to be paid off with fees and consumption metadata.

An open-source tip jar would be useful to some bloggers right now and surely all journalists in the future.
