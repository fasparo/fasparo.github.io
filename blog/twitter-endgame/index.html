Twitter endgame

2014-08-30

Microblogging was invented by Twitter and for this the company deserves some success. Besides, any company endorsed by A-list celebrities and heads of state seems to me a good medium-term investment bet.

But the case against Twitter is strong:

  • The 140-character limit is entirely arbitrary, post-facto excuses notwithstanding. Its very logic is undermined by the varying-length "@"s and "#"s and URLs. The limit functions as a crutch for indiscipline, but not all writers are undisciplined.

  • Plain-text links are surely Twitter's original sin. Their inevitable consequence was link shorteners, which have spent the last few years breaking the web. A discrete URL field would have solved the problem at source.

  • Unlike e-mail, Twitter is proprietary rather than open-source. And unlike RSS it is site-based rather than distributed.

The first two flaws are technical and the company will surely end up fixing them. But the last is intractable.

One day Twitter will be disrupted by an open standard. Its only choice will be whether to fight destiny or to become part of the solution.
