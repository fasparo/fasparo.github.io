Ditch the quote

2013-10-27

According to received wisdom, email is obsolete. Partly for intractable technical reasons relating to security. But also because it encourages people to compose messages like 19th-century letter-writers, with lots of "Dear so-and-so" and "Best regards", when they should be firing off laconic one-liners and generally getting to the point.

As a result, modern email clients look like chat apps. Messages are fully displayed in threads and users are urged to "quick reply" in two-line boxes underneath each message. The threading is assisted by two headers, Message-ID and In-Reply-To, which enable clients to understand the relationship between individual messages.

So why are we still including

17 previous messages in nested quotes

underneath each e-mail?

All this now-useless quoting does not help email's plodding reputation. Even if the quotations are hidden, they must be responsible for a huge proportion of email's bandwidth and carbon footprint.

Let's be pragmatic and surrender to the chattification of email. The first step is to turn off automatic quoting.
