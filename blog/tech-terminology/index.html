Prisoners of tech terminology

2013-02-16

Why talk in trademarks – iPad, iPod, iPhone, iTunes, Kindle, Twitter, Skype, Outlook, Powerpoint, GDoc, Facebook and Google – when generic words are available?

Tablet, music player, phone, music store, e-reader, microblog, chat, spreadsheet, mail, doc, social network and search – do these terms mean less, or are they just less cool? (RSS, an almost-trademark, is not even cool. Perhaps it would be more popular if they had just gone with "web feed".)

I always make an effort to use the generic vocab. It's semantically transparent (what would an "iPod" be if you didn't know already?). It's public, it's open. Above all, it's a subtle declaration of independence, of confidence that these companies and products will one day be replaced by things better. Better for us – as consumers with choice, but also as citizens with autonomy.
