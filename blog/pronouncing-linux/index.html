Pronouncing Linux

2013-07-21

What a mess Linus Torvalds made when naming his kernel. According to the rules of English phonics, it should be pronounced "laɪnəks" ("lye-nooks"). But since many English speakers never absorbed phonics, a lot of people were soon calling it "lɪnəks" ("linnooks", which would be "linnux" under phonics). Linus at first gave his blessing to this latter, incorrect pronunciation (which is the most popular), before explaining that he himself pronounces it something like "liːnʉks" ("leenooks'") – the way it is said in every language but English. I suggest we copy him. English just isn't up to the job.
