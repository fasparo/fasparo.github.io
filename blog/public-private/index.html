Neither public nor private

2014-07-15

By now everyone accepts that if you post to a blog or Twitter, your thoughts are public, forever. Likewise, we all agree that it would be Completely Unacceptable for a webmail provider to leak your email to anyone at all, let alone the general public.

But for many, it seems, the concept of limited sharing is uncomfortably complicated. "Anything you post on the internet is public", say the more outspoken sceptics. Others denounce Facebook for its weasely approach to privacy and then conclude that we would be dumb to expect better. Cynicism added to muddled thinking.

The ambiguous area between public and private speech is what makes the internet special. "Few-to-few" communication – perhaps it could be rebranded "confidential" or "semi-public" – had never been possible before. It should be defended and promoted.
