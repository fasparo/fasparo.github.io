Delete the social button

2015-06-27

Social buttons do not belong on webpages. They are ugly, slow, a pain for developers to maintain, and in theory superfluous. Sharing, liking, tweeting, annotating, upvoting and downvoting are all pure meta. The tools they require are content-transverse and have no place in the content layer.

What would it take to get the buttons into the browser toolbar, where they belong? After all, Google is not going to install a Facebook button in Chrome without a fight.

Enter Firefox, the browser's browser. Mozilla should create and maintain an independently-verified registry of social networks, ranked according to their audited traffic and user numbers. For each of the leading usual suspects Firefox should add a native button to its toolbar, perhaps with dropdown functionality for different verbs (upvote, share, and so on). Then it should proceed to block the corresponding scripts for page-based buttons.

Firefox users would thus get a speed boost and a more beautiful web. And perhaps, just perhaps, that would be enough to get the corporate browsers to copy.
