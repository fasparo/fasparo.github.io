Filter versus search

2013-12-22

America has a great tradition of corporate euphemism. Lobbyists are "public affairs consultants". Used cars are "pre-owned", and even "pre-loved".

But on the web "search" is still "search". This seems odd. The prospect of "searching" for something is hardly one to lift the soul. It sounds like a drudge to me. After all, people don't go to Google to "search". They go there to find!

It is time "search" was euphemized to "filter", since that is what it is. Google could be rebranded a "web filtering company". A euphemism which is also technically correct â€“ that would be a first.
